# Follow-ups dialog

<p style="font-weight: bold;"><span style="font-weight: bold;">[Type]</span></p>
<p>Click the arrow next to this field ( <img src="StandardCRM/bitmaps/Dialogboksknapper_pil ned.bmp" 
 alt="" style="border: none;" 
 border="0" /> ) to display 
 a predefined list of follow-up types to choose from, for example, a meeting 
 or outgoing phone call. You can also search for types by typing into this 
 field. Here you can also use <a href="StandardCRM/chap01/Using_FastSearcher.htm">FastSearcher</a>.</p>
<p class="Note"><span style="font-weight: bold;">Note:</span> It is the 
 <span style="font-weight: bold;">[Type]</span> field which determines 
 the type of follow-up in question, i.e. an appointment, task or phone 
 call.</p>
<p>&#160;</p>
<p style="font-weight: bold;">[Video meeting]</p>
<p>Click <img src="StandardCRM/bitmaps/videomeeting_inactive.png" alt="" 
 border="0" /> to set up a video meeting. The icon changes 
 to <img src="StandardCRM/bitmaps/videomeeting_active.png" alt="" style="border: none;" 
 border="0" />. See <a href="StandardCRM/chap02/video_meetings.htm">Set 
 up video meetings</a>.</p>
<p>&#160;</p>
<p style="font-weight: bold;">[Company]</p>
<p>Enter here the name of the company linked to the follow-up (optional). 
 If you cannot remember the whole name, you can search directly from this 
 field.</p>
<p>&#160;</p>
<p style="font-weight: bold;">[Contact]</p>
<p>Click the arrow next to this field and a list of known contacts in the 
 company is displayed. If no company has been selected, you will see a 
 list of contacts that are not associated with a company. You can search 
 directly from this field.</p>
<p>&#160;</p>
<p style="font-weight: bold;"><nobr style="white-space: nowrap;">Project</nobr></p>
<p>Enter the name of the project that the follow-up is linked to. <span>If 
 you cannot remember the whole name, you can search directly from this 
 field.</span></p>
<p class="Note"><span style="font-weight: bold;">Note:</span><span> In 
 the </span><span style="font-weight: bold;">Project</span><span> field, 
 you can specify any of the projects which are saved in <?rh-udv_start 
 name="SO_User" ?>SuperOffice CRM<?rh-udv_end ?>, regardless of which 
 company the follow-up is linked to.</span></p>
<p>&#160;</p>
<p style="font-weight: bold;">Sale</p>
<p>Here you enter the name of the sale, if any, that the follow-up is linked 
 to.<span> If you cannot remember the whole name, you can search directly 
 from this field.</span></p>
<p>&#160;</p>
<p style="font-weight: bold;">[Time zone selector]</p>
<p><span>If time zones have been activated in SuperOffice Settings and 
 maintenance, you can select which time zone the follow-up will be created 
 in. The time zones are displayed by country, and you can search both by 
 country and city directly in the time zone field (see <a href="StandardCRM/chap06/Time_zones.htm">Time 
 zones</a>).</span></p>
<p>&#160;</p>
<p style="font-weight: bold;"><nobr style="white-space: nowrap;">Start 
 / End/Due / Duration</nobr></p>
<p>Enter a date and, where appropriate, a time in the <span style="font-weight: bold;">Start</span> 
 field and the <span style="font-weight: bold;">End</span> field (for appointments 
 and phone calls) or <span style="font-weight: bold;">Due</span> field 
 (for tasks), for the follow-up start and the follow-up end/deadline respectively. 
 If you specify a time, the content of the <span style="font-weight: bold;">Duration</span> 
 field is automatically adjusted accordingly.</p>
<p class="Note"><span style="font-weight: bold;">Tip:</span><span> If the 
 follow-up conflicts with another one or has several participants, the 
 </span><span style="font-weight: bold;">First available</span><span> field 
 is displayed, showing the first available meeting time.</span></p>
<p>&#160;</p>
<p style="font-weight: bold;">All day</p>
<p>If you check <span style="font-weight: bold;">All day</span>, the time 
 of the appointment is set to the period specified as the default working 
 day.</p>
<p class="Note"><span style="font-weight: bold;">Tip:</span> These default 
 values are specified in the <a href="StandardCRM/chap01/Preferences.htm" 
 style="font-weight: normal;">Preferences</a> 
 dialog. Select <span style="font-weight: bold;">Diary</span>, and enter 
 the required times for <span style="font-weight: bold;">All-day start 
 time</span> and <span style="font-weight: bold;">All-day end time</span>.</p>
<p>&#160;</p>
<p style="font-weight: bold;">Location</p>
<p>Here you choose the address where the follow-up will take place. By 
 default, this field is empty, but if you click the arrow ( <img src="StandardCRM/bitmaps/Dialogboksknapper_pil ned.bmp" 
 alt="" style="border: none;" border="0" /> ) next to the field, two 
 entries are displayed in a list box. One is the user's company and the 
 other is the company that is to be followed up. If you have specified 
 a resource of type <span style="font-weight: bold;">Location</span> as 
 a participant in the <span style="font-weight: bold;">Participants</span> 
 tab, e.g. a meeting room, the resource is also displayed in this list 
 box.</p>
<p class="NoteInBody"><span style="font-weight: bold;">Tip:</span> You 
 can also simply enter an address for the location.</p>
<p style="font-weight: bold;">&#160;</p>
<p style="font-weight: bold;">Completed <img src="StandardCRM/bitmaps/followup_notcompleted.png" 
 alt="" style="border: none;" 
 border="0" /> <img src="StandardCRM/bitmaps/followup_completed.png" 
 alt="" style="border: none;" border="0" /></p>
<p>Check here when the follow-up is completed. <span>For more information, 
 see <a href="StandardCRM/chap02/Marking_activities_as_completed_not_completed.htm">Mark 
 activities as completed/not completed</a>.</span></p>
<p>&#160;</p>
<p style="font-weight: bold;">Task <img src="Bitmaps/btn_Menu.png" alt="" 
 style="border: none;" border="0" /></p>
<p>Click this button to access relevant tasks that can be performed on 
 the active entry. Examples: Delete, Print, Send as e-mail, Assign to and 
 Cancel meeting. In SuperOffice Settings and maintenance, you can also 
 define your own tasks and add them under the <span style="font-weight: bold;">Task</span> 
 button. For more information about this, see the Help for SuperOffice 
 Settings and maintenance.</p>
<p>&#160;</p>
<p style="font-weight: bold;"><span style="font-weight: bold;">Registered/Last 
 modified</span></p>
<p>These fields show the date when the follow-up was first created and 
 when it was last modified. They also show who completed these actions.</p>
<p class="Note"><span style="font-weight: bold;">Tip:</span><span> If you 
 hold the mouse pointer over this text, a tooltip appears containing more 
 information about when the follow-up was created and last modified.</span></p>
